Chapter 04 — When Streaming Hit

Three killers, one outcome

The CD's collapse wasn't one event — it was three. Each disruption handed revenue to a format that paid far less per listen.

1999

Napster

Free file sharing. CD sales fell 20% in two years. Labels won the lawsuit. The habit stuck.

2003

iTunes Store

$0.99 per song vs $15 per album. Per-unit revenue collapsed. The album era ended.

2011

Spotify US

All music for $9.99/month. CD sales cratered. Revenue per stream: ~$0.004.

Chapter 05 — The Paradox

More listeners.
Less money.

Streaming has 600 million users. Music is everywhere. But total U.S. music revenue in 2019 was still less than half its 2000 peak — because the per-listen payout is a fraction of what a CD sale used to generate.

The disc is gone.
The record lives on.

The compact disc ruled for two decades and funded the most profitable era in music history. It's now nearly extinct — outsold by the format it once buried: vinyl. The music industry survived. The CD didn't.
